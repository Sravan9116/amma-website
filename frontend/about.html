Early Life & Biography

Mata Amritanandamayi Devi, affectionately known as Amma, is a world-renowned spiritual leader, humanitarian, and visionary. Born on September 27, 1953, in a small fishing village in Kerala, India, her early life was marked by profound compassion and selfless service. From a young age, she displayed an innate understanding of the suffering of others, often sharing her food and comforting those in need.

Even as a child, Amma exhibited extraordinary empathy and a deep connection to the divine. She spent hours in meditation and contemplation, experiencing profound states of spiritual awareness. Despite facing hardships and challenges, her unwavering faith and commitment to serving others remained steadfast.

In her late teens and early twenties, Amma began to embrace people who came to her seeking solace and guidance. This simple act of compassion evolved into the unique form of darshan (blessing) for which she is now known worldwide â€“ a loving embrace. Over the years, Amma has embraced millions of people from all walks of life, offering them comfort, healing, and inspiration.

Recognizing the need for organized efforts to alleviate suffering, Amma founded the Mata Amritanandamayi Math (MAM), a global network of charitable and humanitarian organizations. Through MAM, Amma has launched numerous initiatives focused on providing food, shelter, healthcare, education, and disaster relief to those in need. Her "Embracing the World" campaign has touched the lives of countless individuals and communities around the globe.

Amma is also the founder of Amrita University, a multi-campus university known for its commitment to research and innovation for social good. The university offers a wide range of academic programs and conducts cutting-edge research in areas such as sustainable development, healthcare, and technology.

Throughout her life, Amma has received numerous awards and accolades in recognition of her humanitarian work and spiritual leadership. She has addressed the United Nations, the World Parliament of Religions, and other international forums, sharing her message of love, compassion, and selfless service.

Today, Amma continues to travel the world, offering her darshan and inspiring people to embrace their own potential for love and compassion. Her teachings emphasize the importance of seeing the divine in everyone and serving all with humility and dedication. Amma's life is a testament to the power of love to transform individuals and communities, and her legacy will continue to inspire generations to come.

Philosophy & Teachings

Amma's teachings are rooted in the principles of love, compassion, and selfless service. She emphasizes the importance of seeing the divine in everyone and serving all with humility and dedication. Amma's philosophy transcends religious boundaries, promoting a universal message of unity and harmony among all beings.

Central to Amma's teachings is the concept of "Seva" or selfless service. She encourages her followers to engage in acts of kindness and compassion, not only as a means of helping others but also as a path to spiritual growth. Through seva, individuals can cultivate empathy, humility, and a deeper connection to the divine.

Amma also emphasizes the importance of meditation and prayer as tools for inner transformation. She teaches simple meditation techniques that can be practiced by anyone, regardless of their spiritual or religious background. According to Amma, regular meditation helps individuals develop inner peace, clarity, and a greater sense of purpose in life.

Another key aspect of Amma's philosophy is the cultivation of love and compassion in everyday life. She encourages her followers to practice kindness, forgiveness, and understanding in their interactions with others. By embodying these qualities, individuals can create a more harmonious and compassionate world.

Amma's teachings also highlight the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of living in harmony with nature. She advocates for environmental conservation and sustainable living as essential components of spiritual practice. Amma believes that by caring for the environment, we are also caring for ourselves and future generations.

Overall, Amma's philosophy is a holistic approach to life that integrates spiritual practice with social responsibility. Her teachings inspire individuals to lead lives of love, compassion, and service, ultimately contributing to the betterment of humanity as a whole.

Vision

Amma's vision is rooted in the principles of love, compassion, and selfless service to humanity. She envisions a world where everyone has access to basic necessities, where people live in harmony with nature, and where love and compassion are the guiding forces in all interactions. Amma believes that by awakening the inherent love and compassion within each individual, we can collectively create a more peaceful and sustainable world. Her teachings emphasize the importance of seeing the divine in everyone and serving all with humility and dedication.

Milestones

  • Birth and Early Years: Born in a small fishing village in Kerala, India, Amma's early life was marked by deep compassion and an innate understanding of the suffering of others. From a young age, she displayed extraordinary empathy, often sharing her food and belongings with those in need. Her spiritual journey began early, with profound experiences of divine love and a natural inclination towards meditation and contemplation.
  • Start of Darshan (Hugging) & Global Outreach: In the 1980s, Amma began offering darshan (a blessing) to people who came to seek solace and guidance. Her unique form of darshan, a loving embrace, became her signature, drawing people from all walks of life. As word spread, Amma's outreach expanded globally, with regular visits to countries around the world, offering comfort and inspiration to millions.
  • Founding of Amrita Institutions: Recognizing the need for organized efforts to alleviate suffering and promote well-being, Amma founded a network of charitable and educational institutions under the banner of "Embracing the World." These institutions include Amrita University, a multi-campus university known for its commitment to research and innovation for social good; Amrita Hospitals, providing affordable and quality healthcare to the poor; and numerous other initiatives focused on disaster relief, environmental conservation, and empowerment of women and children.
